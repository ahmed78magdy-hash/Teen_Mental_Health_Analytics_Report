💡 Business & Research Insights
01
Severe Depression Class Imbalance
Only 2.58% of teens (31/1,200) are labeled depressed. Predictive models require SMOTE or weighted loss; raw accuracy is misleading.
02
Addiction is the Highest Risk Metric
Average addiction score is 5.56/10 — the highest of all measured variables. Ages 17–19 show the highest addiction levels system-wide.
03
System-Wide Sleep Deficit
Average sleep is 6.45h vs. the recommended 8–10h. 240 teens (20%) sleep under 5 hours — a critical risk threshold for mental health.
04
Platform Type ≠ Risk Level
Instagram, TikTok, and "Both" users show near-identical stress and anxiety scores. Duration of use matters far more than platform choice.
05
Ages 15–16 Have Highest Depression Rate
This cohort shows 3.50% depression rate — highest across all age groups — and the highest average anxiety (5.79/10). Priority intervention target.
06
Depression Absent Below Stress Level 7
Zero depressed teens were recorded at stress levels 1–6. All 31 cases cluster at levels 7–10, offering a clear screening threshold.
07
Physical Inactivity is a Compound Risk
34 teens (2.8%) report zero physical activity. Combined with high screen time, this compounds anxiety and addiction risk significantly.
08
Gender Differences Marginal
Females show slightly higher anxiety (5.69 vs 5.59) and depression rate (2.91% vs 2.28%). Males score marginally higher on addiction (5.64 vs 5.49).
09
Pre-Sleep Screen Time is Concerning
Average 2 hours of screen use before sleep. Teens using screens heavily pre-sleep report lower total sleep and higher anxiety levels.
10
High-Volume Segment: Ages 17–19
This group represents 42% of all records (504 teens) and carries the highest average addiction scores. Large volume + high addiction = top priority segment.
